Child Safety Standards

Effective date: 07/12/2026

Dumhawk ("we," "us," "our") operates the mobile application Only Floofs (the "App"), a community for sharing photos of pets. These are our published standards against child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). They apply to everything on the App and to everyone who uses it.

Zero tolerance for CSAE

We prohibit any content or behavior that sexualizes, exploits, endangers, or harms children. This includes child sexual abuse material (CSAM), grooming, sextortion, trafficking, and any attempt to use the App to contact, solicit, or harm a minor. Violations result in immediate content removal and account termination, and are reported to the appropriate authorities.

How the App is designed for safety

  • Pet-only content. Only Floofs exists to share photos of pets. Every uploaded photo passes automated moderation before it can appear publicly, including checks that the image shows an animal and screening for unsafe or sexual content. Content that fails is never published.
  • No private messaging. The App has no direct or private messaging between users, which removes the most common channel for grooming and solicitation.
  • Not directed to children. The App is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly allow accounts held by children under 13.
  • Text moderation. Pet names, captions, and other user text pass automated checks before publication.

In-app reporting

Every photo and profile in the App can be reported from within the App (Report on any post or profile). Reports are reviewed promptly, and content or accounts that put a child at risk are removed. You can also reach us any time at support@dumhawk.com.

Reporting to authorities

We comply with applicable child safety laws. If we become aware of CSAM or of a child in danger, we preserve the material as required by law, remove it from the App, and report it to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC) or the appropriate regional and national authorities, and we cooperate with law enforcement requests.

Point of contact

Our designated child safety point of contact can speak to our CSAM prevention practices and compliance: support@dumhawk.com.

Changes to these standards

We may update these standards from time to time. We will update the effective date above when changes are made.
